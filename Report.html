NOC Guidelines

Guidance on how to obtain NOC

To obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for groundwater extraction in India, follow these general guidelines based on the context provided:

1. Check Eligibility: Ensure that your industry, infrastructure project, or mining activity falls within the categories that require an NOC for groundwater extraction.

2. Online Application: The entire process of obtaining an NOC is typically online through a web-based application system. Visit the Central Ground Water Authority (CGWA) or the concerned State/UT Ground Water Authority's website to access the application portal.

3. Documentation: Prepare the necessary documents required for the application process. These may include:
- Details of water requirement as per the National Building Code, 2016.
- Affidavit confirming non/ inadequate availability of public water supply.
- Certificate of non-availability of water from local government water supply agency.
- Water quality data if extracting saline groundwater.
- Rainwater harvesting plan.
- Impact assessment report for certain projects.

4. Submission: Submit the completed application along with the required documents through the online portal. Ensure all information is accurate and up to date.

5. Compliance: Ensure that your project complies with the guidelines and conditions set by the CGWA or State/UT Ground Water Authority. This may include installing water flow meters, rainwater harvesting systems, and other specified measures.

6. Fees: Be prepared to pay any applicable fees or charges for the groundwater abstraction as per the rates mentioned in the guidelines.

7. Renewal: If you already have an NOC and need to renew it, apply for renewal at least ninety days before the expiry date. Provide a compliance report and any additional documents required for renewal.

8. Monitoring: After obtaining the NOC, ensure compliance with the conditions mentioned in the certificate. Regularly monitor water usage, quality, and any other specified parameters.

9. Extension: If you need an extension for any reason, apply for an extension of the NOC within the specified guidelines.

10. Contact Authorities: If you have any specific questions or need clarification on the process, reach out to the CGWA or the State/UT Ground Water Authority for guidance and assistance.

By following these steps and adhering to the guidelines set by the regulatory authorities, you can successfully obtain a No Objection Certificate for groundwater extraction in India.

Training Opportunities

Training opportunities related to groundwater in India are offered by various government agencies, research institutions, and universities. These programs are designed for professionals, students, and individuals interested in the management, conservation, and sustainable use of groundwater resources. Here are some key organizations and programs that offer training in this field:

1. Central Ground Water Board (CGWB):
- Offers regular training programs on groundwater management, hydrogeology, modeling, and resource assessment.
- Organizes workshops and seminars on topics like artificial recharge, rainwater harvesting, and groundwater quality management.

2. National Institute of Hydrology (NIH):
- Provides specialized short-term training courses on groundwater hydrology, modeling, remote sensing applications, and water resource management.
- Tailored training programs can be arranged for organizations and professionals.

3. Ground Water Department of Various States:
- Many state groundwater departments offer training programs focusing on state-specific issues, regulation, and management practices.
- Provides hands-on field training for groundwater surveying and monitoring.

4. Indian Institute of Technology (IITs):
- Offers professional development programs and short-term courses in water resources management, including groundwater.
- Provides research-based training for advanced studies in groundwater hydrology and related fields.

5. TERI (The Energy and Resources Institute):
- Conducts training workshops on groundwater governance, management, and sustainable water use practices.
- Offers capacity-building programs targeted at policy-makers, government officials, and NGOs in the water sector.

6. Centre for Science and Environment (CSE):
- Provides online and in-person training programs on water management, including courses on groundwater conservation and rainwater harvesting.
- Offers specialized courses focusing on urban water management, groundwater recharge, and usage.

7. International Water Management Institute (IWMI):
- Offers training programs on integrated water resource management, including groundwater management as a key component.
- Collaborates with Indian institutions to offer region-specific training programs.

8. National Water Academy (NWA):
- Provides training in various aspects of water management, including groundwater management, watershed development, and hydrological modeling.
- Programs are aimed at engineers, hydrogeologists, and water resource managers.

9. Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU):
- Offers distance learning courses in water resource management, including modules on groundwater.
- Provides flexible learning options suitable for working professionals.

These training programs and courses offer valuable opportunities to enhance skills and knowledge in groundwater management, contributing significantly to better water resource management in India.

Ground Water Management Practices

Groundwater management practices involve a systematic approach to sustainably manage and conserve groundwater resources. Here are some key details of groundwater management practices:

1. Water Audits:
- Conducting water audits to measure water balance, consumption, and identify opportunities for water savings.
- Analyzing water consumption patterns, water quality, and developing water balance reports for facilities.

2. Recycling and Reuse:
- Implementing recycling and reuse strategies to maximize water efficiency.
- Using treated wastewater for various purposes and putting water to multiple uses.

3. Rainwater Harvesting:
- Implementing rainwater harvesting systems at micro-watershed scale for future use or groundwater recharge.
- Utilizing techniques like roof-top harvesting to collect and store rainwater.

4. Demand-Side Management:
- Setting norms for water budgeting and modernizing industrial processes to reduce water consumption.
- Encouraging water conservation practices and efficient water use.

5. Supply-Side Measures:
- Enhancing aquifer recharge through techniques like rainwater harvesting and urban wastewater reuse.
- Implementing engineering measures to improve groundwater storage and availability.

6. Compliance and Monitoring:
- Ensuring compliance with regulatory guidelines and monitoring water usage, quality, and discharge.
- Developing strategies for water treatment, reuse, and direct use based on monitoring data.

7. Water Balance Analysis:
- Developing detailed water balance reports for facilities to understand water consumption and wastewater generation patterns.
- Identifying specific water-saving opportunities and proposing methods for their implementation.

8. Investment and Implementation:
- Estimating the investment required for implementing water management practices.
- Providing a full description of proposed measures, figures, and detailed implementation plans.

9. Water Quality Management:
- Ensuring proper processing of effluents to adhere to disposal norms and prevent groundwater pollution.
- Monitoring water quality loads and discharges to maintain groundwater quality.

10. Training and Capacity Building:
- Providing training programs for professionals, stakeholders, and individuals on groundwater management practices.
- Enhancing skills and knowledge through workshops, seminars, and specialized courses on water resource management.

By implementing these groundwater management practices, industries, infrastructure projects, and mining activities can contribute to sustainable water use, conservation, and effective groundwater resource management.

Definition of GroundWater Terms

Here is the Glossary of technical terms used in the context of groundwater management practices:

1. Safe Area: An area categorized as safe from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

2. Semi-Critical Area: An area categorized as semi-critical from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

3. Critical Area: An area categorized as critical from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

4. Over-Exploited Area: An area categorized as over-exploited from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

5. Aquifer: A geological formation capable of storing and transmitting groundwater.

6. Deeper Aquifer: In areas with multiple aquifer systems, the aquifer(s) occurring below the uppermost aquifer.

7. Well: Any structure used for the extraction of groundwater, including open wells, dug wells, bore wells, tube wells, filter points, collector wells, infiltration galleries, recharge wells, or any of their combinations or variations.

8. Government Agency: A Central or State Government body responsible for groundwater management.

9. Supplier: Government or government-approved Water Supply Agency providing water services.

10. Mine: An area where mining activities are taking place or an area abandoned after mining.

11. Illegal Groundwater Abstraction Structure: An energized abstraction structure like a dug well, tube well, or bore well used to withdraw groundwater without a valid No Objection Certificate.

12. Rainwater Harvesting: The technique or system of collecting and storing rainwater at a micro-watershed scale for future use or groundwater recharge.

13. Mining Project: A project involving mining activities, either open-cast or underground, or both.

These terms provide a foundational understanding of the key concepts and terminology related to groundwater management practices and resource assessment.
