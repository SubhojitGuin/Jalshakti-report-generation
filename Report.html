NOC Guidelines

Guidance on how to obtain NOC

To obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for groundwater extraction in India, follow these general guidelines based on the provided context:

1. Eligibility Check:
- Determine if your industry, infrastructure project, or mining activity falls under the categories that require an NOC for groundwater extraction.

2. Online Application:
- The entire process of applying for an NOC is online through a web-based application system. Visit the Central Ground Water Authority (CGWA) or the concerned State/UT Ground Water Authority's website to access the application portal.

3. Documentation:
- Prepare all necessary documents required for the application process, such as water requirement details, affidavits confirming non-availability of public water supply, certificates from local government water supply agencies, and any other relevant approvals.

4. Submission:
- Submit the application along with the required documents through the online portal as per the guidelines provided by the CGWA or the State/UT Ground Water Authority.

5. Compliance Conditions:
- Ensure that your application complies with the specific conditions mentioned for obtaining an NOC, such as installation of water flow meters, sewage treatment plants, rainwater harvesting systems, and other relevant measures.

6. Renewal and Extension:
- If you already have an NOC, ensure timely renewal by applying at least ninety days before the expiry date. For extensions, provide valid reasons and necessary documentation to support the request.

7. Monitoring and Compliance:
- After obtaining the NOC, adhere to the monitoring requirements, such as installing water level monitoring systems, conducting water quality tests, and submitting compliance reports as per the specified timelines.

8. Penalties and Charges:
- Be aware of the penalties and charges associated with non-compliance or violations of the NOC conditions, including ground water abstraction charges and environmental compensation.

9. Engage Authorized Officers:
- In case of any issues or queries related to the NOC process, engage with the Authorized Officers appointed by the Central Ground Water Authority or the State/UT Governments for guidance and assistance.

10. Review and Renewal:
- Regularly review the NOC conditions and ensure timely renewal to avoid any disruptions in groundwater extraction activities.

Remember to refer to the specific guidelines provided by the Central Ground Water Authority or the respective State/UT Ground Water Authority for detailed instructions on the NOC application process and compliance requirements.

Conditions for obtaining NOC for ground water extraction

To obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for groundwater extraction in India, the following conditions need to be met based on the provided context:

1. Eligibility Criteria:
- All new/existing industries, industries seeking expansion, infrastructure projects, and mining projects abstracting groundwater are required to seek an NOC from the Central Ground Water Authority or the concerned State/UT Ground Water Authority.

2. Online Application Process:
- The entire process of obtaining an NOC is online through a web-based application system. Applicants need to submit their requests through the designated online portal.

3. Documentation Requirements:
- Applicants must submit specific documents with their application, including details of water requirements, affidavits confirming non-availability of public water supply, certificates from local government water supply agencies, and water quality data from accredited laboratories for saline groundwater extraction.

4. Compliance with Guidelines:
- Applicants must adhere to the guidelines provided by the Central Ground Water Authority or the State/UT Ground Water Authority, including the installation of water flow meters, sewage treatment plants, rainwater harvesting systems, and other specified conditions.

5. Renewal and Extension Process:
- NOCs need to be renewed periodically, and applicants must apply for renewal at least ninety days before the expiry date. In case of delays or genuine reasons for extension, applicants can apply for an extension with proper documentation.

6. Monitoring and Compliance:
- Proponents are required to comply with monitoring requirements, such as installing water level monitoring systems, conducting water quality tests, and submitting compliance reports as per the specified timelines.

7. Penalties and Charges:
- Non-compliance with NOC conditions may result in penalties and charges, including ground water abstraction charges and environmental compensation based on the quantum of groundwater extraction.

8. Engagement with Authorities:
- Applicants should engage with Authorized Officers appointed by the Central Ground Water Authority or the State/UT Governments for guidance, enforcement, and grievance redressal related to groundwater extraction.

9. Review and Renewal Process:
- Regular review of NOC conditions and timely renewal are essential to ensure continued compliance with groundwater extraction regulations.

These conditions outline the necessary steps and requirements for obtaining an NOC for groundwater extraction in India. Applicants should carefully follow the guidelines and provide all the relevant documentation to facilitate the application process.

Ground Water Management Practices

Groundwater management practices involve a range of strategies and techniques aimed at sustainable use, conservation, and protection of groundwater resources. Here are some key aspects of groundwater management practices based on the provided context:

1. Water Audits:
- Conducting water audits is a systematic process to obtain a water balance by measuring water flow from withdrawal or treatment sites through distribution systems to where it is used and discharged.
- Water audits involve preliminary surveys and detailed assessments to identify water use patterns, inefficiencies, and opportunities for water savings.

2. Water Balance and Mapping:
- Developing a detailed water balance of the facility helps in understanding water consumption and wastewater generation patterns.
- Mapping specific water use areas aids in identifying opportunities for water recycling and reuse.

3. Water Saving Opportunities:
- Identifying specific water-saving opportunities within the facility is crucial for reducing water consumption and improving efficiency.
- Implementing strategies for water treatment, reuse, and direct use can lead to significant water savings.

4. Norms and Modernization:
- Setting norms for water budgeting and modernizing industrial processes to reduce water consumption are essential practices in groundwater management.
- Implementing recycling systems, such as re-circulating cooling systems, can help in conserving water resources.

5. Effluent Processing and Compliance:
- Proper processing of effluents to meet disposal norms is critical for protecting groundwater quality.
- Adhering to regulations and guidelines for effluent treatment and disposal is necessary for sustainable groundwater management.

6. Rainwater Harvesting and Reuse:
- Encouraging rainwater harvesting and reuse practices can help in augmenting water resources and reducing dependency on groundwater.
- Utilizing wastewater for non-potable purposes like gardening can contribute to water conservation efforts.

7. Monitoring and Measurement:
- Monitoring water quality loads, discharges, flow rates, and quality parameters is essential for assessing the impact of water use on groundwater.
- Using pressure and flow meters, along with other monitoring devices, helps in quantifying water use and identifying inefficiencies.

8. Investment and Implementation:
- Developing detailed water audit reports with investment requirements and implementation plans is crucial for effective groundwater management.
- Investing in water-saving technologies and infrastructure is key to sustainable groundwater practices.

By implementing these groundwater management practices, industries and organizations can contribute to efficient water use, conservation, and protection of groundwater resources. Regular monitoring, compliance with regulations, and continuous improvement are essential for sustainable groundwater management.

Definition of GroundWater Terms

Here is the Glossary of technical terms used in the provided context:

1. Safe area: Area categorized as safe from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

2. Semi-critical area: Area categorized as semi-critical from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

3. Critical area: Area categorized as critical from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

4. Over-exploited area: Area categorized as over-exploited from the groundwater resources point of view based on the latest groundwater resources assessment.

5. Aquifer: Geological formation capable of storing and transmitting groundwater.

6. Deeper Aquifer: Aquifer(s) occurring below the uppermost aquifer in areas with multiple aquifer systems.

7. Well: Any structure used for the extraction of groundwater, including open wells, dug wells, bore wells, tube wells, filter points, collector wells, infiltration galleries, recharge wells, or any combinations.

8. Government Agency: Central or State government body responsible for groundwater management.

9. Supplier: Government or government-approved water supply agency.

10. Mine: Area where mining activities are taking place or abandoned after mining.

11. Illegal Groundwater Abstraction Structure: Energized abstraction structure like dug wells, tube wells, bore wells used to withdraw groundwater without a valid No Objection Certificate.

12. Rainwater Harvesting: Technique or system of collecting and storing rainwater for future use or groundwater recharge.

13. Mining Project: Project involving mining activities, either open-cast or underground.

These terms provide a foundational understanding of groundwater management concepts and practices as outlined in the context provided.
